Densities and heat capacities per unit volume of the ternary system: water + sodium dodecylsulfate (NaDS) + isopropanol 

(iPrOH) have been measured as a function of concentration of alcohol and surfactant at 298.15 K. Apparent and partial molar 
volumes and heat capacities of isopropanol have been derived therefrom. These quantities reflect the behavior of alcohol in 
the solution. The results confirm that in the dilute region, iPrOH is partially solubilized in the micelles and contributes to the 
formation of mixed micelles, and that in the concentrated alcohol region, iPrOH is solubilized in microaggregates of alcohol. 
The variation of apparent heat capacity at infinite dilution of iPrOH as a function of NaDS concentration shows the existence 
of a transition in the micellar structure around 0.3 m.

A bibliographical study of the thermal decomposition of the ethyl radical shows that the kinetic parameters for the upper and 
lower limits of the pressure are rather scattered. This observation explains why we have carried out a new study of this 
elementary process in the pyrolysis of ethane, around 800 K, in a closed vessel and at pressures of the hydrocarbon varying from 
1 to 300Torr. The experimental results have been treated according to three theoretical approaches: the RRKM theory and two 
methods proposed by Troe. In each case, an excellent fit of the theoretical curves with the experimental data is obtained, as well 
as Arrhenius expressions for the reaction rate constants at high and low pressures. A detailed critical analysis of our results as 
well as those found in the literature shows that as yet there is no good agreement between recent studies on this subject.

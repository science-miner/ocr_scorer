In acid media, metatungstic acid very slowly decomposes into tungstic oxide, an insoluble high polymer; under the same 
conditions, the polymerization of molybdic acid, prepared by ion exchange from the molybdate, NazMo04, is limited ( n = 24). 
The addition of metatungstic acid to molybdic acid involves the relatively rapid formation of soluble high polymers. These are 

mixed molybdotungstic acids whose compositions vary according to the molar fraction, f = [Mo]/[Mo + W], of the reaction 

mixture. When 0.80 S f c 0.95, this reaction is complete: this is a method of obtaining colloids very rich in molybdenum since 

the yield in high polymer is equal to 80%. The formation of the mixed acid can be due either to the direct interaction of the two 
acids or to the decomposition of the metatungstic acid, substituted by the molybdenum, which would be formed transiently. As 
determined by ultracentrifugation and by ion exchanges, the relative proportions of Mo and W in the mixed acids do not vary 
during the course of the polymerization.

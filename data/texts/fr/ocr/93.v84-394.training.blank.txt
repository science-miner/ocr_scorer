correspondant au lieu de se dicarboxyler en phenyl-4 butanol comme les termes inferieurs ou suerieurs; cette difference de

JEAN-YVES NEDELEC, JACQUES FOSSEY, DANIEL LEFORT, and JEANINE SORBA. Can. J. Chem. 62, 2317 (1984). 

5-Phenyl pentanoic peracid decomposes in boiling cyclohexane solution to the corresponding acid instead of decarboxylation 
as is observed for the related cases. This difference in behaviour is explained by the cyclization of the 4-phenyl butyl radical 
which can then no longer propagate any chain reaction leading to 4-phenyl butanol. The peracid, therefore, reacts by a 
competitive radical chain mechanism leading to the corresponding acid.
